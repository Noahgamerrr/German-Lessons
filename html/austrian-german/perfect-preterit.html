Perfekt statt Präteritum

(The perfect instead of the preterit)

27th June 2021

In German, the usage of the preterit has declined from time to time, and it is still declining. What has started in High German dialects like the Austro-Bavarian dialects or the Alemannic dialects (Swiss German, Swabian and the dialects spoken in Vorarlberg, Austria) spread through the mainstream media throughout the entire German-speaking population. In some places more, in some places less. In Swiss German for example, the preterit has completely fallen out of use.

Since we are concentrating here on the Austrian and the Bavarian dialects, we will still need to have a look at both tenses.

Los gehts:

Perfekt (perfect) This tense is mainly used in daily speech. While in Germany, it is completely fine to use the preterit in everyday life, in Austria and Bavaria however, it sounds a little bit unnatural. Most of the time, Austrians and Bavarians use the perfect instead.

Note that people in Germany also rather tend to use the perfect tense.

Example:

Standard German: Gestern Abend ging ich eine Runde spazieren und beobachtete dabei den Sonnenuntergang.

Austrian German: Gestern Abend bin ich eine Runde spazieren gegangen und habe dabei den Sonnenuntergang beobachtet.

English: Yesterday evening, I went for a walk and watched the sunset.

However, there are also exceptions to when to use the preterit in everyday speech.

Präteritum (preterit): This tense is mainly used in writing. People do not regularly use it while talking to each other, except for the verbs “sein”, “haben”, and modal verbs (Like “wollen”, “dürfen”, “müssen” etc.).

Example:

Standard German: Ich war gestern in der Schule und schrieb dort mein Abitur.

Austrian German: Ich war gestern in der Schule und habe dort die Matura geschrieben.

English: I was at school yesterday and wrote my final exams.

(Also note the differences in vocabulary: Germany: “Abitur”, Austria: “Matura”)

However, it is not very uncommon that the perfect is also used with those verbs.

Example:

Ich bin gestern in der Schule gewesen und habe dort die Matura geschrieben.
