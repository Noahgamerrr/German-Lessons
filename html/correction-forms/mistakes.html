Correction of the "Mistakes (A1-B2)" form

29th October 2021

In this week, I made a form where I had again written some faulty sentences and you had to correct them. There were 8 participants with an average of 4,19/7 points (59,82%). Now the only thing left is to correct the sentences. Let’s start with the first one

  1. Ich hoffe, ihn wiedersehen

This is a mistake that many people who learn German make. They forget the “zu” in the sentence, so the sentence is:

  • Ich hoffe, ihn wiederzusehen.

RULE: If there is a dependent clause without a subject, which can be left out if the subject has already been stated in the main clause, you need to use the so-called “zu + Infinitiv”. Also keep in mind that usually, the “zu” part comes before the infinitive. However, if the infinitive is composed of two words (“wiedersehen” is composed of “wieder” and “sehen”), then the “zu” part comes in between those two words.

  1. Ich wohne in dieses Haus.

As most of you have answered correctly, the wrong case is used here. Instead of the accusative, we need the dative. So correctly it would be:

  • Ich wohne in diesem Haus.

RULE: With the preposition „in“: If it states where something is happening, you need to use the dative case: Ich wohne in diesem Haus. However, if a motion is implied, you need to use the accusative case: Ich gehe in dieses Haus. (The verb “gehen” here implies a movement, you aren’t in a place, you are moving to a place)

  1. Peter glaubt mich nicht.

Again, the mistake here lies in the case. “Mich” should be again in the dative, not in the accusative.

  • Peter glaubt mir nicht.

  1. Die Baum ist sehr groß.

This is one of the most basic mistakes you could do in German: Here, the article is wrong. “Baum” is not a feminine noun, it’s a masculine one:

  • Der Baum ist sehr groß.

RULE: There are some rules that make it easier for you to learn the gender of specific words. However, those rules don’t count for every single word, so unfortunately, you have to memorize the gender of each word separately.

  1. Seit zwei Monate lerne ich Deutsch.

Here, again, has the wrong case been used. Instead of the accusative, the dative should have been used. Hence:

  • Seit zwei Monaten lerne ich Deutsch.

RULE: You can consult this page again, where you can take a look at which case you should use when. However, I think it wouldn’t be uninteresting to see the declension of each gender in the German language.

masculine feminine neuter plural (m/f/n)
nominative der Mann die Frau das Auto die Monate
genitive des Mannes der Frau des Autos der Monate
dative dem Mann der Frau dem Auto den Monaten
accusative den Mann die Frau das Auto die Monate
  1. Ich spiele mit dem Hase.

This one was probably the trickiest. Because, yes, after “mit”, the dative has to be used, but what is wrong here, is that the word “Hase” has been declined the wrong way. Actually, it should be:

  • Ich spiele mit dem Hasen.

RULE: The word „Hase“ is part of the so-called “n-declension”. All the words that are in this declension share these characteristics:

  • Masculine nouns, with the only exception of “das Herz” (the heart)
  • People or animals
  • Plural form has to end with “-n” or “-en”

The declension goes as follows:

singular plural
nominative der Hase die Hasen
genitive des Hasen der Hasen
dative dem Hasen den Hasen
accusative den Hasen die Hasen

However, it is also worth to point out, that this declension is dying in common speech. Many people tend to not say the ending so:

  • Ich sah den Studenten

becomes:

  • Ich sah den Student.

  1. Er sagte mir, dass ich soll zu ihm kommen

This mistake here has to do with how the dependent clause has been built. Because the word order is messed up. Correctly, it should be:

  • Er sagte mir, dass ich zu ihm kommen soll.
  • Alternatively (sentence from @fowa): Er hat mir gesagt, ich soll zu ihm kommen.

RULE: When a dependent clause starts with a conjunction (dass, weil, da, etc.), the word order in that clause changes from SVO to SOV. Or put in another way: The inflected verb slides to the end.
