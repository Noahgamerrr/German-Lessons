Political parties in Austria

09th August 2021

In Austria, there are five major parties having seats in the Parliament, the so-called “Nationalrat”. The parliament gets reelected every five years and with it the chancellor as well.

Those five parties are: (ordered by the number of seats)

Party Ideology Chairman Seats Similar party in Germany
Österreichische Volkspartei (ÖVP) Conservatism Alexander Schallenberg 71 CDU/CSU
Sozialdemokratische Partei Österreichs (SPÖ) Social democracy Pamela Rendi-Wagner 40 SPD
Freiheitliche Partei Österreichs (FPÖ) Right-winged populism Herbert Kickl 30 AfD
Die Grünen – Die Grüne Alternative (GRÜNE) Green politics Werner Kogler 26 Bündnis 90/Die Grünen
NEOS – Das Neue Österreich und Liberales Forum (NEOS) Liberalism Beate Meinl-Reisinger 15 FDP

As you might see on this graphic, there is a seat “oK”. This stands for “ohne Klubzugehörigkeit” (without membership to any party) and it’s reserved to Pia Philippa Strache who was elected back then as a member of the FPÖ but who was restricted to have a seat for this party by then party chairman Norbert Hofer and by then club chairman Herbert Kickl because of the Ibiza-affaire caused by her husband Heinz-Christian Strache (then FPÖ).
