Passive voice

28th January 2022

The German language distinguishes between two voices: The active and the passive voice. While in the active voice, the subject does an action, in the passive voice, the subject receives an action. This distinction does, of course, exist in English as well, like for example:

Example: The house has been sold to a couple.

Now, we want to take a look at how this passive voice is built in German.

Vorgangspassiv vs. Zustandspassiv

German makes a quite peculiar distinction in its passive voice. The so-called "Vorgangspassiv" is used when you want to emphasize the process, while the "Zustandspassiv" is used when you want to emphasize the result. So, in German, the phrase:

Example: The house was sold yesterday.

Can be translated as one of those two options:

Das Haus wurde gestern verkauft. (Vorgangspassiv)

Das Haus war gestern verkauft. (Zustandspassiv)

Passive voice in the Präsens

In the present-tense, the Vorgangspassiv is constructed with the copula "werden" and the main verb in the past participle:

Conjugation of "werden"
werden
ich werde
du wirst
er wird
wir werden
ihr werdet
sie werden

Example: Das Auto wird gerade verkauft.

The car is just being sold.

The Zustandspassiv on the other hand is conjugated with the copula "sein", instead of "werden"

Conjugation of "sein"
sein
ich bin
du bist
er ist
wir sind
ihr seid
sie sind

Example: Das Auto ist verkauft.

Passive voice in the Präteritum

The passive voice for the preterit is just as easy as for the present. There, you just need to put the copula "werden" or "sein" in it's preterit tense.

Conjugation of "werden" and "sein"
werden sein
ich wurde war
du wurdest warst
er wurde war
wir wurden waren
ihr wurdet wart
sie wurden waren

Example: Das Auto wurde gestern verkauft. (Vorgangspassiv)

Das Auto war gestern verkauft. (Zustandspassiv)

The car was sold yesterday.

Passive voice in the Futur I

Now, the passive constructions are getting a bit more complicated. In the future I, you need the conjugated "werden" in the present tense + the main verb in the past participle + the copulas "werden" or "sein" in the infinitive.

Example: Die Insel wird in 10 Jahren überflutet werden (Vorgangspassiv)

Die Insel wird in 10 Jahren überflutet sein (Zustandspassiv)

The isle will be flooded in 10 years.

Passive voice in the Perfekt

In the perfect tense, the passive is constructed with the present form of "sein" + the past participle of the main verb + the past participle of the copulas "werden" (Vorgangspassiv) or "sein" (Zustandspassiv), which are "worden" (in this particular case, there is also "geworden") and "gewesen".

Example: Gestern ist meine Brieftasche gestohlen worden. (Vorgangspassiv)

Gestern ist meine Brieftasche gestohlen gewesen. (Zustandspassiv)

My wallet was stolen yesterday.

Passive voice in the Plusquamperfekt

In the pluperfect tense, we build the passive the same as the perfect, only that the verb "sein" at the beginning will be conjugated in the past tense, not the present tense.

Example: Gestern war meine Brieftasche gestohlen worden, bevor ich nach Hause ging.

(Vorgangspassiv)

Gestern war meine Brieftasche gestohlen gewesen, bevor ich nach Hause ging.

(Zustandspassiv)

My wallet had been stolen yesterday before I came back home.

Passive voice in the Futur II

Last but not least the future II. In that specific tense, you conjugate the passive voice with the present tense of "werden" + the past participle of the main verb + the past participle of the copulas "werden" (Vorgangspassiv) or "sein" (Zustandspassiv) + the infinitive of the verb "sein".

Oof, that's a lot to digest. So let's try to make it clearer with an example.

Example: Bis dahin wird die Hausübung schon erledigt worden sein. (Vorgangspassiv)

Bis dahin wird die Hausübung schon erledigt gewesen sein. (Zustandspassiv)

Till then, the homework will have already been finished.

I think it might be helpful if we go through those sentences step by step:

"Bis dahin" just means "till then", then we see "wird", which is the 3rd person singular present of "werden" and is part of the passive construction. Next, we see "die Hausübung" which means just "the homework" and "schon" which just means "already". Next up, there is "erledigt", which is the past participle of "erledigen" (to finish). The next word we see is the past participle of "werden" and "sein", which make the difference between the Vorgangspassiv and the Zustandspassiv. Last but not least, there is the verb "sein". And that's it. That's all you have to know about the passive voice.
