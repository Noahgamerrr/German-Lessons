Comparative - Superlative

12th April 2022

If we want to express that one quantity is greater than the other, we use the comparative forms of adjectives. The superlative is used for quantities that are greater than all the others. And today we want to take a look at how German deals with these inflections.

Comparative (Komparativ)

The comparative is formed by adding the "-er" suffix to the adjective, or just an "-r", if they end in an e. If the root of the adjective containes an "a", "o" or "u", these vowels often get changed to their Umlautforms.

Example: groß - größer

klein - kleiner

krank - kränker

Superlative (Superlativ)

For the superlative, you need the word "am" followed by the adjective that has the "-sten" suffix added. there are two things to note:

  1. The vowels in the stem change again to their Umlaut form.
  2. If the adjective itself ends with an s-sound, then it only gets the suffix "-ten" added.

Example: groß - am größten

klein - am kleinsten

krank - am kränksten

German wouldn't be German however, if it weren't for any exceptions. So, here are the most important exceptions you need to know:

Irregularities in the Comparative and Superlative forms
Main form Comparative Superlative
gut besser am besten
viel mehr am meisten
hoch höher am höhsten
nah näher am nächsten
hart härter am härtesten
gern lieber* am liebsten*

gern: People in southern Germany and in Austria tend to use "gern - gerner - am gernsten", next to "lieb - lieber - am liebsten."

Ambiguity

Some words have two comparatives: one with the Umlaut, and one without. It's different from speaker to speaker which one gets used.

Irregularities in the Comparative and Superlative forms
Main form Comparative Superlative
blass blasser/blässer am blassesten/am blässesten
from frommer/frömmer am frommsten/am frömmsten
gesund gesunder/gesünder am gesundesten/am gesündesten
nass nasser/nässer am nassesten/am nässesten
schmal schmaler/schmäler am schmalsten/am schmälsten

Participles

Participles are verbs that act like adjectives. They don't have their own comparative form. Instead, they just go along with the word "öfter".

Example: Dieses Lied wird öfter gespielt als alle anderen Lieder dieser Band.

In their superlative-forms however, they get as a prefix words like "meist" or "best" added.

Example: Sein Lied ist das meistgespielte in deutschen Radios.

Comparison of two quantities

Now we will take a look at how to compare two quantities with each other in a sentence. There are three cases which we need to look at:

1. Both quantities are the same.

In this case, we use the phrase "so" +adjective +"wie".

Example: Der blaue Ball ist so groß wie der rote.

(The blue ball is as big as the red one.)

2. One quantity is bigger than the other

Here, we use the adjective +"als".

Example: Der blaue Ball ist größer als der rote.

(The blue ball is bigger than the red one.)

3. Out of all quantities, one is the biggest

In the last case, we use the article + the declined form of the superlative.

Example: Dieses Kleid ist das schönste, welches ich besitze.

(This dress is the most beautiful one which I possess.)
